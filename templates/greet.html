Welcome to our MySQL Case Study!

In this MySQL case study, we embark on an exciting journey into the world of art and data analysis. Leveraging the power of MySQL, we delve deep into a rich dataset encompassing paintings, artists, museums, and more, sourced from the renowned Famous Paintings dataset.

We harness the capabilities of various technologies, including PostgreSQL, PgAdmin4, Pandas, and SQLAlchemy, to conduct an in-depth analysis of the dataset. Our exploration covers a wide range of topics, from data loading and manipulation to insightful SQL queries and analysis.

The SQL queries we craft provide valuable insights into the art world, enabling data-driven decision-making processes and fostering a deeper understanding of the dataset. From identifying popular painting styles to analyzing museum opening hours, each query uncovers unique aspects of the art dataset.

This case study not only showcases the power of MySQL for data analysis but also highlights the intersection of art and technology. Through our exploration, we aim to inspire further research and discovery in the realm of art and data analytics.

Database Schema

The MySQL database schema used in this case study is designed to effectively organize and store the art-related data. It consists of multiple tables, each representing different entities such as paintings, artists, museums, and more.

Here's an overview of the main tables and their relationships:

  • work: Contains information about individual paintings, including their titles, artists, and associated museums.
  • artist: Stores details about artists, such as their names, nationalities, and styles.
  • museum: Represents museums and includes data such as museum names, locations, and operating hours.
  • subject: Records the subjects or themes depicted in paintings.
  • canvas_size: Describes the sizes of canvases used for paintings.
  • product_size: Contains information about product sizes related to paintings, such as sale prices and regular prices.
  • image_link: Stores links to images of paintings.
  • museum_hours: Defines the opening and closing hours of museums on different days.

The relationships between these tables are established through primary and foreign key constraints, ensuring data integrity and enabling efficient querying.

Overall, the database schema provides a comprehensive structure for storing and managing art-related data, facilitating various analyses and insights.

PostgreSQL Queries

These queries provide valuable insights into the art dataset, allowing researchers, art enthusiasts, and analysts to explore trends, patterns, and relationships within the data.

PostgreSQL Queries

Question Query
Retrieve the titles and artists of all paintings created after a certain year. SELECT title, artist FROM paintings WHERE creation_year > {year};
Calculate the total number of paintings in each style category. SELECT style, COUNT(*) AS total_paintings FROM paintings GROUP BY style;
Find the museums with the highest number of paintings on display. SELECT museum_name, COUNT(*) AS total_paintings FROM museums JOIN paintings ON museums.id = paintings.museum_id GROUP BY museum_name ORDER BY total_paintings DESC LIMIT 5;
Determine the average price of paintings in each size category. SELECT size_category, AVG(price) AS average_price FROM paintings GROUP BY size_category;
Identify the most common subjects depicted in paintings. SELECT subject, COUNT(*) AS total_paintings FROM paintings GROUP BY subject ORDER BY total_paintings DESC LIMIT 10;
